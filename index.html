My Design
Process

Every project follows a rigorous five-step cycle that keeps users at the centre and quality at the end.

01

Research

Understand the user's world, the business goals, and the competitive landscape before any sketching begins. Interviews, analytics, and desk research surface the real problem — not the assumed one.

02

Wireframe

Translate insights into structure. Low-fidelity wireframes map the information architecture and user flows — fast, cheap to change, and perfect for aligning stakeholders before committing to visuals.

03

Prototype

Bring the design to life in Figma — interactive flows, transitions, and micro-interactions that simulate the real product. A great prototype answers questions without writing a word of code.

04

Test

Put the prototype in front of real users. Usability sessions, feedback loops, and iteration until the design is genuinely intuitive — not just visually polished.

05

Ship

Hand off with precision — annotated specs, organised Figma files, design tokens, and ongoing QA support. Design doesn't end at delivery; it ends when users are using it successfully.
